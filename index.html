Supporting Haitian-American Youth Kids

Who Is Serge Desir?

A husband, father, coach, chef, mentor and a man of God.The accolades he has would be too extensive to write and the many different hats he wore would be hard to represent but Serge did it well. Serge was a loving husband to Margarette Desir, and father to four Sarah, Elizabeth, Gabrielle, and Serge II. He was the sole proprietor of Gigite Catering Services, serving Haitian-American cuisine to the city of Boston for many years. He was also the founder of Haitian-American Youth Soccer (HAYS) where he was the head soccer coach providing a place for Haitian-American kids to participate on his soccer team from ages ranging eight to-17. He held annual soccer camps in the city of Boston and also had his team participate annually in the summer for Mayor Menino’s Boston Neighborhood Soccer League (BNSL) where the teams had much success. Serge was also a devoted Christian serving his church Tabernacle Baptist Church as Head Treasurer from their start. Serge could be remembered always catering church anniversaries, fellowships and any events he was there to serve God and give back. Again we could write a book on all he has done for the Haitian community. The purpose of this foundation is to remember all he has done for the community and his legacy.

About Us

The main highlight of the foundation is to support a scholarship fund for Haitian-American student-athletes who are planning to further their education in Business and/or Entrepreneurship.

Our Mission

Providing a source of mentorship and community building with the youth of Haitian-American kids who are planning to further their education in Business and/or Entrepreneurship.

Our Plan

Investing in the future of Haitian-American students who aspire to build careers in the business management/entrepreneur industry or whom are student-athletes by providing annual schloarships.

Our Vision

To be able to assist Haitian-American youth with mentorship partnerships and scholarship funds. Give back to the youth of the Haitian-American community of Boston. Please apply here to learn more.

Our Team

Elizabeth Desir - Founder and CEO

Elizabeth Desir, known to family and friends as Liz was thinking of a way to possibly give back to the community the same way her father did. The thought of the foundation came to her as she was chatting with friends and after a conversation with her siblings the foundation was slowly formed. She knew she always wanted to make a difference by one day working with kids. It wasn’t until she was a high school assistant coach that she realized the need for some form of mentorship for other Haitian-American kids. “I just wanted to find a way to give back to this particular demographic of youth. I noticed while being a coach that Haitian kids often felt misunderstood or didn’t have that form of support they truly needed. I’m very proud of my Haitian heritage and providing a safe space for kids to speak and mentor them is a huge goal of mine”. Liz is an alum of University of Massachusetts, Lowell where she obtained her Bachelor of Science in Business Administration dual major in Management and Marketing in 2011, and was a member of the Women’s basketball team playing at the Division II level. Liz currently works as a Healthcare Administrator in Boston. She also dedicates her time to coaching girls high school basketball at Randolph High School for the last four years. And like her father enjoys cooking and hosting and being around good people.

Sarah Desir - COO and Co-Founder

Sarah Desir, the eldest daughter of Serge Desir, currently works as a Registered Nurse in the Boston area dedicating her career servicing our Veterans. Due to Serge’s extensive medical history being a heart transplant recipient medicine has always fascinated her and she followed in her mother’s footsteps pursuing her nursing degree. “My father is an excellent example of living your best life- he got a second chance by getting a new heart and he did not let any moment go to waste”. Sarah joins her siblings taking pride in honoring her father’s legacy with building this scholarship foundation so that young scholars can know that a Haitian immigrant came to America to build something better for his future and we have to continue that dream.

Gabrielle (Gaby) Desir - CFO and Co-founder

Gaby Desir, the third daughter of Serge Desir, is currently working in Product Development. Gaby studied Fashion Design at Framingham State University; this decision was heavily encouraged by both of her parents. Her mother introduced her to the likes of fashion and sewing as a teenager. As well as her father who truly supported this route of potential entrepreneurship. The foundation is not only to give everyone a piece of Serge Desir throughout the community but to give back with great intention of inspiring the young adults. Allow others to feel the support that I once felt at their age. Hope and a chance is a beautiful thing from adults to young individuals. I am happy to know the Serge Desir Foundation will get that.

Serge Desir II - Marketing Manager

SSerge Desir II, the youngest of the four children and only son, is currently taking after his father’s footsteps with the entrepreneurship and business owning ventures with his Real Estate business in partnership with Keller Williams Realty. Growing up with being exposed to family businesses, church involvement, and community influence, this has translated well into Serge II’s adult life. The ideology of having a positive influence on a community is something political figures aim for, and Serge was able to do just that within his own platforms in an era where your reputation was by word of mouth. This foundation is going to provide a network and proper resources for upcoming youth that will be able to fulfill their dreams with the support of a community behind them.
